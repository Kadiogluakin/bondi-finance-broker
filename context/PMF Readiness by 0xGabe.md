2. Defining PMF in the context of Web3
What is PMF?
Solving a real problem for a defined audience
+ achieving sustainable adoption/revenue.



Key differences of Web3 PMF vs Web2 PMF
1.Growth and Scaling
a.Fungible tokens, airdrops, user incentives
b.User ownership and engagement
c.Partnerships with key industry leaders
2.User Onboarding Experience
a.Self-custody wallets, network gas fees, getting assets on-chain
b.Immutability & decentralization vs centralized systems
c.Underlying blockchain, scalability, available infrastructure
3.Monetization Models
a.Tokenomics
b.Protocol fees

The Problem I Keep Seeing

Founders don’t know what problem they’re solving,
who they’re solving it for,
and attempt to pursue it unsustainably with
over reliance on token incentives.

3. The Roadmap to PMF Readiness

Actionable steps/questions to assess and achieve PMF:
1.Identify the problem and deeply understand your target audience
a.Define the core problem your product solves
b.Understand your competition well (How is your proposed solution better? SWOT Analysis)
c.Understand your user persona (e.g. DeFi traders, non-crypto users, creators/builders etc)

2.Validate your ideas early before getting too invested
a.Get user feedback early to iterate on your MVP
b.Build your MVP as cheaply as possible to understand if there’s a market for your idea
c.Iterate based on what is working well, pivot quickly and cut on what isn’t working.



3.Planning ahead: Business model, funding, sustainability:
a.Identify potential revenue streams for future sustainability if you plan to monetize
b.If you launch a token, weigh different tokenomic designs that aligns incentives between all 
parties (community, team, investors)
c.Plan early how you want to fund your operations and scale growth, and tie that in with your 
fundraise (VC raise? Fair launch? ICO?)

4.Track key metrics to measure PMF progress and refine your approach:
a.Set core metrics for PMF (daily active users, user retention rates, incentives spent to user 
conversion rates, TVL, transaction volume, user referral activity etc)
b.Analyze user behaviour and optimize for what is working well
c.Benchmark your metrics with similar projects to assess relative performance
d.Are users adopting and returning to your product without heavy incentives? Token incentives 
won’t save your product if no one wants to use it



5.Once PMF is evident, scale with confidence without losing your core audience:
a.Leverage network effects
i.Incentivize users to bring other users to your platform (referral programs, community bounties)
ii.Foster integrations with other protocols/partners to expand reach (DeFi composability, 
partnering with market leaders)
b.Expand markets and target audience
i.Moving beyond the early crypto-native audience to mainstream users
ii.Improve onboarding experience to reach new non-crypto users (UIUX)
iii.Working with new distribution channels (CEXs, DEXs, wallet apps, real-world Web2 partners)
c.Invest in ecosystem development
i.Use grants or ecosystem funds to support other developers building on your platform
ii.Co-incentivize new integrations or initiatives with ecosystem partners
iii.Expand current product feature offering in a synergistic manner

4. Common Challenges and Pitfalls in reaching for PMF

●Over-reliance on token incentives for growth
●Misalignment between product vision and user needs
●Premature scaling before validating PMF
●Unsustainable tokenomics and misaligned incentives between market participants
●Bad or unfavorable go-to-market timing

5. Real-world case studies, success stories and learnings

Pendle
What is Pendle?:
●DeFi protocol enabling users to trade tokenized yield and 
principal tokens on-chain, a novel use-case and first of its 
kind.
●E.g. Separating yield and principal from natively yield bearing 
tokens like Lido’s wstETH or Mantle’s mETH.
Current Success:
●At its peak in June 2024, Pendle had ~$6.7 billion TVL due to 
its huge success in the new points and ETH restaking 
narrative.
●Currently has over $5 billion TVL and is the 11th biggest DeFi 
protocol in the industry.

Pendle
Challenges:
●Not many know this but Pendle is a relatively old protocol 
that launched back in July 2021 and had seen little to no 
adoption for almost 3 years.
●Product was too complex and ahead of its time, early 
DeFi users weren’t sophisticated enough to understand it.
●There was no PMF with its v1 despite being a novel idea.
Key Learnings:
●Persistence and belief in what you’re building.
●Market timing when they pivoted to the points and 
restaking market narratives (enabled users to leverage 
their points on pre-token DeFi protocols). From Jan-Jun 
2024, the platform's TVL scaled 30x, reaching $6 billion.
●Iterating based on user feedback for better UIUX.

Hyperliquid
What is Hyperliquid?:
●Decentralized CLOB perpetual exchange which became a 
performant L1 chain optimized for fast onchain trading with 
CEX-like user experience.
Current Success:
●At its peak in Dec 2024, Hyperliquid had ~$3.4 billion TVL due to its 
huge success as the leading decentralized perp DEX with great UIUX.
●Hyperliquid’s HYPE airdrop stands out as the most lucrative airdrop in 
crypto’s history at approximately $7.5 billion (27.5% supply to users) at 
its peak valuation, surpassing that of Uniswap and Arbitrum.
●Hyperliquid currently holds a ~67.7% market share dominance 
compared to other decentralized perp DEXs.
●During the TRUMP token launch, Hyperliquid drove 9.8% and 23.5% the 
24H volume of both Binance and Bybit perps respectively ($19.3 bil vol).

Hyperliquid
Challenges:
●Back when Hyperliquid first launched in 2023, the perp DEX 
sector was highly competitive with over 20 other competitors 
solving the same problem. Standing out against established 
competitors like dydx, Vertex & GMX was difficult.
●Hyperliquid had no VC investors and backers, they had to 
solve liquidity constraints with community support.
Key Learnings:
●Solve key user pain points for seamless user onboarding.
●Align incentives through community-focused tokenomics, 
reward high quality and loyal users. Organic growth.
●Understand and prioritize your primary user base (memecoin 
traders, always the first to list new hyped tokens)

Friend.Tech
What is Friend.Tech?:
●Decentralized social app built on Base that allows 
creators to connect to their audience through tokenized 
attention.
●Purchase ‘keys’ (tokens) to access creators’ private 
chat rooms and gain access to other exclusive perks.
What happened to Friend.Tech?:
●At its peak in Aug 2023, Friend.Tech accounted for more than 
half of all activity on Base as KOLs and users rapidly speculated 
on the product due to its novel idea. Within 2 weeks of its 
launch, Friend.tech rapidly surpassed 100K users and generated 
over $90 million in protocol fees.
●Shortly after its token launch, the FRIEND token plummeted 98% 
due to unsustainable tokenomics and mishandled public 
backlash.
●Friend.Tech’s creators decided to shutdown the app in mid-2024 
amid failed airdrop, stalled growth and product stagnation.

Friend.Tech
Challenges:
●Unsustainable business model / token design driven by continuous 
inflows of new users and speculation (ponzinomics). User 
adoption declined once initial hype wore off and prices of creator 
keys stopped going up.
●Failed to meaningfully innovate beyond its basic functionality.
●Poorly handled public backlash, lost of credibility as initial backers 
started pulling out (Paradigm), community lost faith in the project.
Key Learnings:
●Unsustainable tokenomics and novel ideas may drive speculation 
initially, but it risks blow-up. Over-reliance on speculation.
●Poor communication and mismanagement of expectations will 
erode trust between the project and its users/partners.
●TLDR: Hype alone cannot sustain a platform. Build for sustainable 
growth and driving long-term value.

6. Closing Remarks

5 key takeaways from this workshop:
●Clear value proposition and PMF is the foundation of sustainable growth, over reliance on 
token incentives can’t make up for a shitty product.
●Validate, iterate and align with your users’ needs. Avoid building features that seem "cool" but 
don’t address actual user pain points.
●Tokenomics is a balancing game. Focus on aligning incentives with all market participants 
(users, investors, team) for long-term growth and value creation.
●Community is key in Web3. Focus on user retention, not just acquisition. Sustainable growth 
comes from engaging with and retaining users. If users don’t stick around, it’s a sign that PMF 
hasn’t been achieved yet.
●Stay agile and open to pivoting. The crypto space evolves rapidly and market demands are 
always changing. Achieving PMF is not a one-time milestone, but an iterative process.
